# Source Digest

This digest summarizes the reusable patterns distilled from `research_elite_outputs/elite_output_library.md`.

## Firm Families and What to Steal

1. McKinsey / MGI
   - Turn broad uncertainty into executive decision architecture.
   - Use issue trees, survey evidence, productivity logic, and economic impact sizing.
   - Always end with a managerial "so what."

2. BCG / Henderson Institute
   - Treat strategy as competition under changing conditions.
   - Use capability gaps, transformation readiness, and future-back scenarios.
   - Name the strategic contradiction.

3. Bain
   - Make strategy operational.
   - Connect profit pools, customer economics, implementation sequences, and KPI dashboards.
   - Ask what a PE owner would measure first.

4. Big Four / Accenture
   - Translate trends into enterprise transformation.
   - Include governance, risk, regulation, operating model, and adoption friction.
   - Make the path executable for large organizations.

5. a16z / Sequoia / YC / First Round / Bessemer / NFX
   - Identify non-obvious market creation logic.
   - Ask: why now, what wedge, what distribution path, what compounds?
   - Prefer founder truth and customer pull over abstract market size.

6. Gartner / Forrester / IDC / CB Insights / PitchBook
   - Build taxonomies, maturity curves, category maps, and adoption timelines.
   - Distinguish hype, pilot, deployment, and budget-scale adoption.
   - Make vendor or player comparison systematic.

7. Investment Banks / Asset Managers / Macro Thinkers
   - Connect narrative to money: earnings, margins, multiples, capital flows, M&A, rates, liquidity.
   - Use scenario ranges, downside analysis, and indicators.
   - Ask how the market would price the change.

## Master Framework

Every strong output should answer:

1. What changed?
2. Why now?
3. What is the mechanism?
4. Who captures value?
5. What evidence proves it?
6. What actions follow?
7. What would falsify it?

## Quality Bar

Elite Oracle should feel like a compressed partner meeting:

- fewer adjectives, more distinctions
- fewer trends, more mechanisms
- fewer claims, more evidence
- fewer generic actions, more ownership and sequencing
- fewer conclusions, more decision-ready implications
